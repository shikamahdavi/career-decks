How I Lead

Leadership Story

I lead by making the invisible visible. In learning organizations, the hardest challenge is rarely the training itself. It is proving that training matters, knowing where the gaps are before they show up in customer experience, and building the measurement infrastructure that turns "we think it works" into "here is exactly how it performs."

At Schwab, I led this work across a large, matrixed enterprise. I partnered with operations leaders, workforce management, product teams, and technology partners to align learning investments with the metrics that mattered: resolution rate, handle time, NPS, and associate readiness. I built reporting ecosystems that gave senior leaders real-time visibility, not quarterly summaries.

I also believe that technology is only as good as the strategy behind it. I have owned learning technology roadmaps end to end, evaluated AI-driven coaching tools, and implemented knowledge management systems. But I always start with the business question: what do we need associates to know, how do we know they know it, and what changes when they do?

Selected Work

Achievements & Case Studies

Challenge

Enterprise learning programs lacked a unified measurement framework. Leadership had no reliable way to connect training investments to business outcomes like NPS or handle time.

Action

Designed and implemented a comprehensive evaluation model tying learning interventions to operational KPIs. Built real-time dashboards for senior leadership and partnered with workforce management to calibrate training timing and intensity.

Result

~20% increase in program adoption. Frontline performance improved 15-20%. Leadership gained real-time visibility into training ROI for the first time.

Challenge

Knowledge management across the enterprise was fragmented. Associates spent excessive time searching for answers, driving up handle time and escalation rates.

Action

Led the design and rollout of a centralized knowledge management system with structured content governance. Implemented search optimization and usage analytics to identify and close knowledge gaps proactively.

Result

Reduced support escalations. Measurably faster speed to proficiency for new associates. Knowledge consumption data became a leading indicator for training needs.

Challenge

Learning technology stack was aging and disconnected from business strategy. No roadmap existed for next-generation tools.

Action

Owned the end-to-end learning technology roadmap. Evaluated AI-driven coaching platforms, systems training interfaces, and modern LMS/LXP solutions. Partnered with tech and product teams to align investments with operational efficiency goals.

Result

Delivered a multi-year technology roadmap adopted by senior leadership. Positioned the learning function as a strategic partner in digital transformation.
